INTRODUCTION

iZotope Labs is a playground for iZotope's most adventurous customers and sonic experimentalists to play with, abuse, and give feedback on emerging iZotope product concepts, DSP algorithms, and other stuff we are throwing against the wall.

ABOUT

Welcome, iZotope! Until now, projects like M209 and Vinyl haven’t been released to the world for REASONS! Fortunately, through the creation of iZotope Labs, we’ve created a new brand identity and framework through which we can release all sorts of passion projects while also collecting valuable user feedback. So, do you have an idea or tech that you think the world needs to experience? Then read on and help us share your passion with the world!

Ideators

The Review Crew

This is a rotating 5 person cross-functional team that meets regularly to help approve and schedule iZotope Labs releases. Key functions represented would be Dev, QA, Design, Marketing, and Product Management, along with an iZotope Labs scrummaster.

OUR SUBMISSION PROCESS

How do I make my idea a reality...? This is our best guess right now!
Person

Submission

You’ll be able to submit your project via a web interface and provide essential information that will help it be evaluated by the Review Crew. Your project might be in one of two categories: a Mini-Product (slightly more polished) or an Experiment (unpolished R&D). You’ll need a binary or link to a functional mini-product or experiment, along with basic instructions and/or a ReadMe. You’ll also need to work up a name and product description...but fear not, you can always get a ColLABorator to help you!

Mobile

Review

Fortunately for you, the review process is meant to be as frictionless as possible. Projects do not require installers or polished UIs. Projects will not be officially supported by iZotope, so OS/host compatibility is also not a factor. However, the Review Crew is present to be the final check that the product works, and that you’ve provided essential information. If it’s a Go, the Review Crew will queue it up for scheduling. If it needs work, it will be bounced back to you with feedback for adjustments.

Desktop

Scheduling and Release

The Review Crew will evaluate bandwidth for iZotope Labs releases. They will meet every 2-3 weeks on a Friday afternoon to vet project candidates and prep/stage releases when necessary. Many factors will weigh into the release schedule, and Mini-Products may have a different release rhythm than Experiments, which may happen more frequently to support product development.

Desktop

Retirement

The Review Crew will continuously evaluate the inventory of iZotope Labs to ensure everything in it is relevant. The Review Crew will also expire Experiments as needed in advance of new launches. In some cases the project will be retired because the work is done, in some cases it will be retired because it has, like Pinocchio, become a REAL THING that iZotope support officially going forward. The process for this evolution is work yet to designed, but will likely involve metrics and reports on iZotope Labs project successes, our product roadmaps, the RMT and PI Planning sessions.
